Bio

Born in 1987, I was the youngest of three children. My parents always taught me the value of hard work, determination, and perseverance. Growing up, I never had a set career path in mind, but I knew that no matter what I put my efforts into, that I would go far. I graduated from Denair High in 2005 and attended Modesto Junior College, where I encountered a menagerie of different walks of life. My world would never be the same. It wasn't until a close friend of mine suggested that I attend a climbing school in Idaho. Northwest Lineman College helped shape my mind and body even further. The rigors that I endured were worth the turmoil and now, I'm expanding my mind-muscles. In the winter of 2020, I began my coding odyssey. The instructor, TA's, and other students have been greatly appreciated and have helped along the way.


I've been a part of great teams and look forward to meeting and working with excellent mentors. Honesty and integrity are values that I do my best to embody, for my own standards and for others... A positive work environment that encourages and develops their employees is ideal. While I know how to work diligently, I make sure to take time for myself. Over the years, I've discovered that most physical activities are a must for me. Country swing dance, hiking, and working out have been great outlets for me to meet new people and burn off some energy. Though I always enjoying being physically active and enjoying life, I know when to buckle down and get work done.


Over the years, I've learned to forge my own path and always have a good time. While I know when to be serious and get things done, I make sure that I maintain my child at heart attitude.
